## 1. Fund Strategy

The Pinnacle Note Fund invests in performing, reperforming, and non-performing residential mortgage notes secured by real property throughout the United States. The fund's strategy is to acquire loans at a discount to unpaid principal balance and generate returns through scheduled cash collections, loan resolutions, and capital appreciation.

The fund operates with two primary investment sleeves:

**Income Sleeve** — Performing and reperforming mortgage notes generating scheduled monthly cashflows. Emphasis on stable, income-producing assets with acceptable LTV profiles and current or near-current payment history.

**Workout Sleeve** — Non-performing and sub-performing mortgage notes where the primary return is generated through loan resolution — reinstatement, modification, short sale, deed-in-lieu, foreclosure, or REO disposition. Requires active asset management and specialized servicer oversight.

---

## 2. Income Sleeve — Definition & Criteria

Loans classified as Income must meet all of the following:

- Performing or reperforming (RPL) status
- At least 6 months of confirmed payment history (for RPLs: at least 6 months post-modification or post-reinstatement)
- Secured by real property in an eligible state
- First lien position preferred; second lien permitted within allocation limit
- LTV at or below the Income Sleeve LTV limit (see `/policies/risk_limits.md`)
- Proper escrow setup (or acceptable alternative for non-escrowed loans)
- No active foreclosure or bankruptcy (RPLs may have prior resolved action)

---

## 3. Workout Sleeve — Definition & Criteria

Loans classified as Workout include:

- Non-performing loans (NPL) — 90+ days delinquent or in foreclosure
- Sub-performing loans — 30–90 days delinquent without a credible cure plan
- Loans in active bankruptcy (Chapter 7, 11, or 13)
- Loans with confirmed collateral impairment or complex legal history

Workout loans require a documented NPL action plan (see `/templates/npl_action_plan_template.md`) within 30 days of acquisition.

---

## 4. Eligible Assets

The following asset types are eligible for acquisition:

- First lien residential mortgage notes (primary strategy)
- Second lien residential mortgage notes (within allocation limit)
- Mortgage notes secured by 1–4 unit residential properties
- Mortgage notes secured by 5+ unit multifamily properties (within allocation limit)
- Mortgage notes secured by condominiums (within allocation limit)
- Reperforming notes (post-modification, subject to payment history requirement)

---

## 5. Ineligible Assets

The following are ineligible without CEO/CIO written exception approval:

- Mortgage notes secured by commercial property (retail, office, hospitality)
- Construction loans
- Reverse mortgages
- Loans with known environmental contamination on the collateral
- Loans in states where the fund's servicer is not licensed or authorized
- Loans with known title defects that are not curable or insurable
- Notes secured by land only (no improvements)
- Consumer notes (unsecured debt, auto, student loans)
- Notes with UPB below the minimum or above the maximum per buy box

## 7. Return Standards

| Sleeve | Target Net IRR | Minimum Net IRR |
|---|---|---|
| Income (Performing/RPL) | 8–12% | 7% |
| Workout (NPL) | 15–20% | 12% |
| Deep Workout / REO | 20%+ | 15% |

Return thresholds are set by the CEO/CIO and may be adjusted based on market conditions. Any acquisition below the minimum IRR requires CEO/CIO written approval with documented rationale.

## 8. Underwriting Standards

All loans must be underwritten using the note underwriting template (`/templates/note_underwriting_template.md`). Minimum underwriting standards:

- Confirmed UPB and payment history
- Confirmed property address and property type
- Collateral value from at least one source (AVM, BPO, or appraisal)
- Confirmed tax status
- Confirmed insurance status or escrow analysis
- Confirmed legal status (foreclosure, bankruptcy)
- Assignment chain review (pre-bid: from tape; pre-close: full document review)
- Note health score assigned (1–10)

Loans with incomplete data may be underwritten with documented assumptions, but all material gaps must be identified and risk-adjusted in pricing.

---

## 9. Diligence Standards

Full diligence must be completed before any acquisition closes. No exceptions. Diligence must include:

- Physical review of original note, mortgage/DOT, all assignments and allonges
- Confirmed recording of all recorded instruments
- Title report or title insurance commitment from approved title vendor
- Tax certificate or county records confirmation
- Insurance confirmation
- Legal status confirmation
- Seller authority documentation (entity authorization, signatory evidence)

Loans with unresolved Critical diligence exceptions may not close without CEO/CIO written waiver.

## 10. Portfolio Construction Rules

- Income Sleeve target: 85% of portfolio by balance at launch; 70–85% long-term operating range
- Workout Sleeve target: 15% of portfolio by balance at launch; 15–30% long-term operating range
- Single-state concentration limit: per `/policies/risk_limits.md`
- Single-seller concentration limit: per `/policies/risk_limits.md`
- Second lien allocation limit: per buy box and risk limits
- Multifamily allocation limit: per buy box

Portfolio construction is monitored by Agent 13 monthly. Any proposed acquisition that would breach a concentration limit requires CEO/CIO approval.
