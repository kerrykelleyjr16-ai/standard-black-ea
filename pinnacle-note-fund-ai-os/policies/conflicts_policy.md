# Conflicts of Interest Policy

**Fund:** The Pinnacle Note Fund
**Effective Date:** [DATE]
**Approved By:** CEO/CIO
**Monitored By:** Agent 15 (Conflicts, Audit Controls & Governance)

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy governs the identification, disclosure, approval, and monitoring of conflicts of interest that arise in the operation of The Pinnacle Note Fund. Conflicts are inevitable in a fund management context — the objective is not to eliminate them but to identify them promptly, disclose them appropriately, manage them fairly, and document everything.

---

## 2. Related-Party Transactions

A related-party transaction is any transaction between the fund (or any SPV) and:
- The fund manager (GP entity or management company)
- Any officer, director, or key person of the fund manager
- Any entity in which the fund manager or key persons hold a material interest
- Any family member of a key person who has a material role in the transaction
- Any investor who also has a service or vendor relationship with the fund

**Policy:**
- All related-party transactions must be identified before they occur
- All related-party transactions require CEO/CIO approval; some require LP consent per the LPA
- All approved related-party transactions must be disclosed to investors in the annual report
- Agent 15 maintains the related-party transaction log

---

## 3. Allocation Conflicts

When the fund's manager (or affiliated entities) manages multiple funds or accounts, allocation conflicts may arise when an investment opportunity is suitable for more than one fund or account.

**Policy:**
- The Pinnacle Note Fund's investment opportunities are allocated solely to this fund during the fund's active investment period (per LPA)
- If an affiliated entity also has a legitimate claim to a specific opportunity, the allocation decision must be documented by CEO/CIO with clear rationale
- All allocation decisions that involve any potential conflict are logged in the conflicts register and disclosed in the annual report

---

## 4. Vendor Conflicts

A vendor conflict exists when a service provider to the fund (servicer, counsel, title company, appraiser, fund admin, auditor) also has a relationship with:
- The fund manager, GP, or key persons
- Any investor in the fund
- Any asset seller the fund transacted with

**Policy:**
- All vendor relationships are screened by Agent 15 for conflicts upon engagement
- Any vendor with a conflict relationship must be disclosed to investors
- The conflicted vendor may remain engaged if:
  - The conflict is not material (defined as: vendor revenue from fund is <30% of vendor's total revenue)
  - CEO/CIO approves the engagement with documented rationale
  - The relationship is disclosed to investors
- Agent 15 reviews all vendor relationships annually

---

## 5. Personal Trading Conflicts

No key person of the fund manager may:
- Purchase for their personal account any mortgage note or related real estate asset that the fund has evaluated or is actively evaluating (blackout period applies for 90 days following the fund's pass decision)
- Short-sell or hedge against the fund's portfolio in their personal account
- Purchase a property that the fund's REO portfolio is in the process of selling

**Policy:**
- Key persons must disclose all personal real estate investment activity to CEO/CIO
- CEO/CIO reviews for potential conflicts
- Any potential personal trading conflict is logged in the conflicts register

---

## 6. Referral Fees and Finder's Fees

Any referral fee, finder's fee, or placement fee paid to any person in connection with an acquisition, investor referral, or service provider introduction must be:

- Disclosed to the fund's investors (in the annual report or a separate disclosure notice if material)
- Approved by CEO/CIO
- Reviewed by Agent 14 (Compliance) for regulatory compliance
- Logged in the conflicts register

No finder's fee arrangement may be entered into without CEO/CIO written approval.

---

## 7. Side Letters

Side letters granted to any investor that provide preferential economic or informational rights must:

- Be disclosed to the fund's legal counsel before execution
- Be reviewed by Agent 15 for consistency with the fund's MFN obligations to other investors
- Be approved by CEO/CIO
- Be disclosed to other investors entitled to MFN treatment under their side letters

Agent 15 maintains a register of all side letters and their material terms.

---

## 8. Approval and Disclosure Process

### Identification
Agent 15 maintains a live conflicts register. All agents, key persons, and vendors are responsible for self-reporting potential conflicts. Early identification prevents bigger problems.

### Approval
- Minor conflict (limited financial impact, no LP consent required): CEO/CIO written approval required
- Material conflict (significant financial impact or LP consent required per LPA): CEO/CIO approval + LP notice and/or consent per the LPA + legal counsel review

### Disclosure
- All approved conflicts are disclosed in the fund's annual report
- Material conflicts that arise between reporting periods may require a special notice to investors (CEO/CIO decision, Agent 14 reviews language)

### Logging
All conflicts identified, approved, and disclosed are logged in:
- `/logs/decision_log.md` — approval decision
- Agent 15's conflicts register (maintained separately)
- Fund's annual report (disclosure record)

---

## 9. Escalation

- Any conflict that cannot be clearly approved at the CEO/CIO level → escalate to fund's legal counsel before action
- Any conflict that may violate the LPA or applicable law → halt action; escalate immediately to CEO/CIO and legal counsel
- Any undisclosed related-party transaction discovered after the fact → immediate disclosure assessment and CEO/CIO decision on LP notification
