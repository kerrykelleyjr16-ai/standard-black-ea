**Created:** 2026-05-31
**Purpose:** The curriculum to learn the HVAC/plumbing roll-up cold — the way the note business was learned via Desi Arnez. Reverse-engineer the proven operators, then run their playbook at a smaller scale.
**Companion docs:** `README.md` (thesis), `references/company-evaluation-frameworks.md` (evaluation discipline), `.claude/skills/acquisition-analyzer/SKILL.md` (deal scorecard).

## Frame Before You Start (your altitude)
You are **not** starting as a private-equity platform. You're a **searcher/operator** building a small regional platform — buy one solid anchor company, then bolt on a few more in one metro. That's a micro-version of what the giants below do.

The key insight: **the big PE platforms are both your model to copy AND your eventual exit buyers.** Build a clean $5–15M-revenue platform with real management and books, and there's a ready list of 27+ consolidators who will buy it from you. Study them to learn the playbook now; sell to them later.

---

## Tier 1 — The Proven Platforms to Reverse-Engineer
Study how these were built. They've spent billions proving what works — copy the pattern, not the scale.

| Platform | Backer | Scale | Why study it |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Apex Service Partners** | Alpine Investors + Partners Group | ~107 brands, ~$1.3B rev, ~300 businesses, 8,000+ techs; ~60 add-ons in 2025 | The #1 model. Largest residential HVAC/plumbing/electrical roll-up. Highest deal cadence in the industry — study their add-on machine. |
| **Wrench Group** | Ares Management | Reportedly sold ~$14B (2021) | The blockbuster outcome story. Read the case critically (some circulated numbers/attributions are unverified — focus on the *strategy*). |
| **Champions Group** | Blackstone | Acquired ~$2.5B at ~18.5x EBITDA | Proof of the multiple arbitrage — bought small shops, sold the platform at a premium multiple. |
| **Sila Services** | Goldman Sachs ($1.7B) | 30+ companies, Northeast/Mid-Atlantic | Regional density model — exactly your "own one metro first" approach, scaled. |
| **ARS / Rescue Rooter** | GI Partners + Charlesbank | Among largest US residential HVAC/plumbing | Long-running brand consolidation playbook. |
| **Redwood Services** | — | ~$1.1B (2025 recap) | Newer platform — current-cycle tactics. |

**What they ALL do (the repeatable playbook):**
- Buy small shops at **4–6x EBITDA**; the consolidated platform sells at **8–12x** → the spread is the profit (multiple arbitrage).
- Add-on screen: within **60–90 miles** of existing operations, **5–10+ service vehicles**, **management stays post-close**, **strong maintenance-agreement base**, **no customer overlap**.
- Value creation after the buy: consolidate back-office, install dispatch software, push **recurring membership/maintenance programs**, fund marketing + technician recruiting, enforce pricing discipline.
- Residential HVAC is "midway through the consolidation cycle"; commercial is still early.

## Tier 2 — The Operator-Level Curriculum (your actual playbook)
This is where *you* learn to do a deal — searcher scale, not PE scale.

1. **Acquiring Minds podcast** (host: Will Smith) — **the single best free resource.** 2x/week interviews with real people who bought HVAC, plumbing, and other small businesses. Tactics, deal structures, mistakes, financing. Start with the HVAC and plumbing episodes.
   - Apple Podcasts: https://podcasts.apple.com/us/podcast/acquiring-minds/id1569715379
   - Episode list: https://acquiringminds.co/episodes
2. **Book: *Buy Then Build* by Walker Deibel** — the bible for acquisition entrepreneurs ("acquire instead of start from scratch"). Forbes top-7 entrepreneur read. Read this first.
3. **Walker Deibel's Acquisition Lab** — paid done-with-you buy-side program if you want structured support later (defer until activation).
4. **Case study — first-time plumbing acquisition** (Charles Barr / DiMartino Plumbing, via Acquiring Minds) — a real first plumbing buy: financials, deal structure, lessons.

## Tier 3 — Market & Financing Reality (the facts to anchor on)
- **~145,000+ HVAC companies** in the US, most under **$10M revenue** — deep, fragmented supply of targets.
- **$50B+ residential market**, mid-single-digit growth; total HVAC (incl. commercial/manufacturing) **$350B+**.
- **PE add-on acquisitions up ~88% YoY** through mid-2025 — you're entering a hot, validated market (competition at the larger end; the small end is still open).
- **Recession resistance** comes from recurring maintenance contracts + non-discretionary repair demand.
- **Financing:** SBA 7(a) works up to **~$5M** purchase price — your likely entry lane for the anchor deal. Above that, or for active roll-ups, you need middle-market/PE financing. At the small end, lean on **seller financing + earnouts** (lowers capital at risk, keeps sellers honest).

## Recommended Study Sequence (next 60–90 days, near-zero cost)
1. **Week 1–2:** Read *Buy Then Build*. Get the acquisition-entrepreneur mindset.
2. **Week 2–6:** Acquiring Minds — every HVAC/plumbing episode. Take notes on deal structures and what went wrong.
3. **Ongoing:** Build the **comps file** — log every HVAC/plumbing business for sale (BizBuySell, brokers): price, revenue, EBITDA, multiple, location.
4. **Ongoing:** Reverse-engineer **Apex** specifically — read everything on how they screen and integrate add-ons.
5. **By end:** Run **3–5 real listings** through the seven-step evaluation order (`company-evaluation-frameworks.md`) and log them in `decisions/log.md`. Reps build instinct.
6. **Decide:** Confirm the **first target metro** (default: DFW).
