# Skill: Market Analysis

## Trigger
Use this skill when Kerry needs to analyze a market — car sales, mortgage notes, or business acquisition targets. Delivers structured findings with actionable insights.

## Inputs Needed
- Lane: car market, note market, or business acquisition
- Geography: city, state, region, or national
- Specific question: what decision does this analysis need to support?
- Data sources available (optional — I'll identify sources if not specified)

---

## Three Analysis Lanes

---

### Lane 1: Car Market
Use when Kerry needs to understand local inventory, pricing, competition, or buyer demand at Clay Cooley VW.

**Key questions this lane answers:**
- What are comparable vehicles selling for nearby?
- What inventory is moving vs. sitting?
- Who are the direct competitors in Richardson/DFW area?
- What should Kerry be pitching based on what's hot?

**Data sources:**
- Cars.com, AutoTrader, CarGurus (public pricing and inventory)
- Facebook Marketplace (local deals and buyer behavior)
- Manufacturer incentive pages (VW monthly deals)
- Local competitor dealership websites (inventory pricing)

**Analysis output format:**
```
## Car Market Analysis — [Date]

**Geography:** [Area]
**Vehicle focus:** [Make/Model/Segment]

### Competitive Pricing
| Vehicle | Mileage | Competitor | Price | Days Listed |
|---|---|---|---|---|

### Market Observations
- [What's moving fast]
- [What's sitting]
- [Price trends]

### Recommended Positioning
[How Kerry should pitch against market — price angle, feature angle, service angle]
```

---

### Lane 2: Note Market
Use when Kerry needs to understand mortgage note pricing, geography, deal availability, or seller/servicer landscape.

**Key questions this lane answers:**
- What are performing notes trading at in a given region?
- Which states have favorable foreclosure timelines?
- Who are active sellers and brokers in this space?
- What does current tape look like on the market?

**Data sources:**
- PaperStac, NotesDirect (public note marketplace listings)
- FCI Lender Services, Madison Management (servicer intel)
- State foreclosure law guides (ATTOM, RealtyTrac, state court data)
- Note investor forums and groups (BiggerPockets Notes, Facebook groups)

**Analysis output format:**
```
## Note Market Analysis — [Date]

**Geography:** [State(s)]
**Focus:** [Performing / Reperforming / Non-Performing]

### Pricing Observations
- Current bid-to-value range: [X%–Y%]
- Yield expectations in market: [X%–Y%]

### State Foreclosure Overview
| State | Process | Avg Timeline | Risk Level |
|---|---|---|---|

### Active Sellers / Brokers
[Any identified sources or contacts]

### Market Opportunity Assessment
[What the data suggests for Kerry's buy box and sourcing strategy]
```

---

### Lane 3: Business Acquisition
Use when Kerry is evaluating a sector for operating company acquisition — HVAC, plumbing, construction, trucking, property management.

**Key questions this lane answers:**
- What does the competitive landscape look like in a target sector?
- What are businesses in this sector typically valued at?
- What are the demand drivers and risks?
- What size and geography makes sense for a first acquisition?

**Data sources:**
- BizBuySell, BizQuest (business listing prices and multiples)
- IBISWorld industry reports (sector revenue, trends, major players)
- Google Maps / Yelp (local competitor density)
- SBA loan data (deal structures and valuations)

**Analysis output format:**
```
## Business Acquisition Market Analysis — [Date]

**Sector:** [HVAC / Plumbing / Construction / Trucking / Property Mgmt]
**Geography:** [Target area]

### Sector Overview
[2–3 sentences: size, growth, demand drivers]

### Valuation Range
- Typical EBITDA multiple: [X–Y x]
- Typical asking price range for target size: [$X–$Y]

### What Good Targets Look Like
[Based on acquisition scorecard from portfolio playbook]

### Red Flags to Avoid
[From scorecard]

### Competitive Landscape in Target Geography
[Density, fragmentation, major players]

### Recommendation
[Whether to pursue, what size/geography to start, next step]
```

---

## General Workflow

1. Confirm lane and specific question
2. Identify the right data sources for the analysis
3. Gather and structure findings
4. Build the analysis output in the format above
5. Lead with the most actionable insight — don't bury it

---

## Output Format
Table + narrative hybrid. Lead with the executive summary (3 bullets max), then the full findings, then recommendations. Always tie back to what Kerry should do differently based on the data.
