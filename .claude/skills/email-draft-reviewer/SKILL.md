# Skill: Email Draft Reviewer

**Invoke when:** Any email is about to be sent — whether drafted by Kerry, Claude, or pulled from a template. Nothing external goes out unreviewed.

---

## Inputs
- Draft email (paste the full text)
- Recipient type (investor / LP / note seller / servicer / broker / vendor / team / personal)
- Purpose of the email (intro / follow-up / deal inquiry / offer / legal / general)
- Any context about the relationship

## Workflow

### Step 1 — Approval Gate Check
Does this email touch any of these?
- Investor or LP → STOP. Requires Kerry's explicit approval before sending.
- Legal or contract-related → STOP. Requires Kerry's review.
- Offer, bid, or financial commitment → STOP. Requires Kerry's approval.
- Borrower or servicer (active deal) → Flag for review.

### Step 2 — Tone Check
Does the email match the recipient?
- External / business: Direct, professional, genuine southern hospitality — no stiffness, no hollow jargon
- Investor / institutional: Confident, credible, institutional quality — no hype
- Team / internal: Direct, plain, no corporate softening
- First contact: Warm, specific about why you're reaching out, clear ask

### Step 3 — Content Check
- Does it say what it needs to say — and nothing more?
- Is the ask clear? (What does Kerry want them to do?)
- Any promises, commitments, or representations that shouldn't be in writing?
- Any sensitive information that shouldn't be in email? (Account numbers, borrower PII, deal terms before approval)
- Is it too long? (Most external emails should be under 150 words)

### Step 4 — Standard Black Voice Check
- Does it sound like Kerry? (Confident, direct, plain language, big ambitions stated plainly)
- No filler phrases: "I hope this finds you well," "As per my last email," "Please don't hesitate"
- No over-qualification or hedging
- Sign-off: -KJ for casual, Kerry Kelley Jr for formal

### Step 5 — Output
Return:
- Revised draft (clean, ready to send) OR
- Flag list of issues that require Kerry's attention before sending

## Output Format
Revised draft + brief note on what changed (2–3 bullets max). Flag approval requirements clearly at the top.
