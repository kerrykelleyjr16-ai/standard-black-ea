# Skill: Acquisition Analyzer

**Invoke when:** Kerry is evaluating a potential operating company acquisition — HVAC, plumbing, construction, trucking, or property management.

---

## Inputs
- Business name / type / location
- Revenue and EBITDA (if available)
- Asking price or deal structure
- Why Kerry is looking at it

## Target Sectors (Phase III+)
- HVAC
- Plumbing
- Commercial construction
- Trucking / logistics
- Property management

## Screening Criteria (Quick Disqualifiers)
Stop early if:
- Revenue under $500K (too small for the model)
- No clear owner-operator or management to retain
- Industry in structural decline
- Located in a geography with no growth thesis
- Asking price over 5x EBITDA without clear justification

## Workflow

### Step 1 — Business Snapshot
Capture:
- What does this business do?
- Years in operation
- Revenue trend (3 years if available)
- EBITDA or owner's discretionary earnings
- Number of employees
- Key customer concentration risk

### Step 2 — Valuation Check
- Calculate EBITDA multiple at asking price
- Compare to sector norms (typically 2.5–4x for trades businesses)
- Estimate post-acquisition EBITDA with Standard Black management
- Identify any quick-win margin improvements

### Step 3 — Acquisition Structure
Evaluate deal options:
- Full purchase (cash or financed)
- Seller financing (preferred — aligns incentives)
- Earnout structure (ties price to performance)
- Management buyout partnership

### Step 4 — Operational Fit
- Does Standard Black have management bandwidth to run this?
- Can Kody or a hired operator take the wheel?
- What systems need to be built or replaced?
- What's the 12-month plan post-close?

### Step 5 — Risk Flags
- Owner dependency (business dies without the current owner)
- Customer concentration (1 client = more than 30% of revenue)
- Deferred maintenance or capex needs
- Regulatory or licensing requirements

### Step 6 — Scorecard

### Step 7 — Recommendation
- Pursue / Pass / Revisit in [timeframe]
- If pursuing: next step (call seller, request financials, engage advisor)
- Log decision in `decisions/log.md`

## Output Format
One-page acquisition brief: snapshot, valuation, scorecard, recommendation, next step.
