# Skill: Document Creation

## Trigger
Use this skill when Kerry needs to create a formal document — SOP, memo, outline, one-pager, proposal, or structured report. Delivers a clean, ready-to-use document in Standard Black voice.

## Inputs Needed
- Document type (see types below)
- Audience: internal team, investor, client, legal, public-facing
- Purpose: what this document needs to accomplish
- Key points or raw content to build from (can be rough notes or bullet points)

---

## Document Types

### 1. SOP (Standard Operating Procedure)
For: repeatable processes that the team needs to follow consistently.

**Format:**
```
# [Process Name] — SOP

**Owner:** [Name]
**Effective Date:** [Date]
**Version:** 1.0

## Purpose
[1–2 sentences: what this SOP governs and why it exists]

## Scope
[Who this applies to]

## Steps
1. [Step] — [Details]
2. [Step] — [Details]
...

## Notes / Exceptions
[Any edge cases or important caveats]

## Review Schedule
[When this SOP should be reviewed and updated]
```

---

### 2. Business Memo
For: internal communication — decisions, policy, updates, direction to the team.

**Format:**
```
MEMO

To: [Recipients]
From: Kerry Kelley Jr
Date: [Date]
Re: [Subject]

[Opening — state the purpose in 1–2 sentences]

[Body — key information, organized in short paragraphs or bullets]

[Closing — what action is required, if any]
```

---

### 3. Outline
For: structuring a talk, presentation, course, or long-form piece before writing it.

**Format:**
```
# [Title / Topic]

## I. [Section 1]
   A. [Point]
   B. [Point]

## II. [Section 2]
   A. [Point]
      1. [Sub-point]
      2. [Sub-point]
   B. [Point]

## III. [Section 3]
...
```

---

### 4. One-Pager
For: investor introductions, business summaries, deal overviews, or external pitches.

**Format:**
```
# [Company / Deal / Initiative Name]

**Tagline:** [One powerful sentence]

## What It Is
[2–3 sentences — plain English, no jargon]

## The Opportunity
[Why this matters, why now]

## How It Works
[3–5 bullet points covering the model or structure]

## Who's Behind It
[Kerry's background and the team — brief]

## The Ask / Next Step
[What you want the reader to do]

**Contact:** Kerry Kelley Jr | [email] | [phone]
```

---

### 5. Proposal
For: pitching a deal, partnership, service, or initiative to an outside party.

**Format:**
```
# Proposal: [Title]

**Prepared by:** Kerry Kelley Jr, Standard Black
**Prepared for:** [Recipient]
**Date:** [Date]

## Overview
[What is being proposed and why]

## Opportunity
[The problem or gap this addresses]

## Proposed Solution
[What you're offering / what the deal looks like]

## Terms / Structure
[Key terms, economics, timeline]

## Why Standard Black
[Brief credibility statement]

## Next Steps
[Clear call to action with a timeline]
```

---

**Format:**

```
**Purpose:** [One line]

## Executive Summary
[3–5 bullets: the most important takeaways]

[Content]

[Content]

-
-
-

1.
2.
3.
```

---

## Workflow

1. Confirm document type and audience
2. Gather key points or raw input from Kerry
3. Select the right format from above
4. Draft in Standard Black voice — direct, confident, institutional quality
5. Deliver clean, formatted, copy-paste-ready document
6. Offer to adjust tone, length, or format if needed

---

## Output Format
Deliver the complete document, formatted and ready to use. No preamble — just the document. If input is rough, clean it up. If it's thin, ask one follow-up question before drafting.
