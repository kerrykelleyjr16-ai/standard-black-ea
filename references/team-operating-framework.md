## Weekly Operating Rhythm

### Thursday Weekly Check-In — 45 Minutes
*Every Thursday. Operational. Keeps the week on track.*

| Block | Time | Content |
|---|---|---|
| Win review | 5 min | What got done last week — each person shares one |
| Priority alignment | 10 min | What are we focused on this week — Kerry sets the tone |
| Task assignments | 15 min | Who owns what this week — specific, measurable |
| Blockers | 10 min | What's in the way — Kerry resolves or re-routes |
| Close | 5 min | Confirm assignments, set next check-in |

**Format:** Group call (voice). No slides. No lengthy presentations. Direct and to the point.

**Kerry's role in this meeting:** Set the priorities, assign tasks, unblock, and close. He doesn't facilitate — he leads.

### 2nd and 4th Sunday — Strategy & Review — 60 Minutes
*Bi-monthly. Bigger picture. Existing team cadence.*

| Block | Time | Content |
|---|---|---|
| Monthly scorecard (2nd Sunday) | 15 min | Are we on track for the month's goals? |
| Course correction | 15 min | What's off track and why |
| Monthly focus areas | 15 min | Top priorities for the next two weeks — Kerry sets |
| Resource/money | 10 min | Team payment pool update, any financial decisions |
| Close | 5 min | Written summary sent after call |

*4th Sunday is the accountability close — did we do what we said we would do this month.*

## Accountability System

### How Tasks Get Assigned
Every task needs:
1. **Owner** — one name, not "the team"
2. **Deliverable** — what done looks like
3. **Due date** — specific date, not "soon" or "this week"

If a task doesn't have all three, it doesn't exist.

### How Tasks Get Tracked
Until a formal tool is set up: Kerry keeps a running list of who owns what. At every weekly check-in, that list gets reviewed. No assignment falls through without being surfaced.

*Recommended upgrade when ready: Notion or Linear for task tracking. Keep it simple.*

### When Things Don't Get Done
No performance management theater. Kerry asks one question: **What happened?**

Two acceptable answers:
1. It got done — here's the output
2. It didn't get done — here's why and here's the revised plan

One unacceptable answer: silence or deflection.

## Delegation Framework

### What Kerry Should Delegate Now
- Defined, repeatable tasks with a clear playbook (TJ)
- Course progress and note study execution (Kody + TJ)
- Research and data gathering tasks (either)

### What Kerry Keeps Until Further Notice
- All business decisions with money or legal implications
- Investor/partner communications
- Strategic direction and prioritization
- Anything representing Standard Black publicly

### How Kerry Delegates Effectively
1. **Brief the task** — what it is, why it matters, what done looks like
2. **Set the deadline** — specific date
3. **Check the output** — review it, not just confirm it was submitted
4. **Give feedback** — quick debrief after execution builds their judgment

## Communication Protocol

### Internal (Team)
- **Primary:** Text/iMessage and group calls — current default
- **Recommended upgrade:** Telegram — better file sharing, searchability, and future integration potential. Kerry already has it.
- **Rule:** All task assignments confirmed in writing after the call. No verbal-only assignments.

### Decision-Making
- **Kerry decides:** Everything strategic, financial, and external
- **Kody executes:** Operating tasks assigned by Kerry
- **TJ executes:** Defined playbook tasks assigned by Kerry
- **Escalation:** If anyone is blocked, they come to Kerry same day — not at the next check-in

### Conflict/Pushback
Disagreements happen in the meeting — not after it. If someone has a concern, raise it in the room. Once a decision is made, everyone aligns and executes.
