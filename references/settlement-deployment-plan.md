## Phase 1 — Pre-Marriage Window (Settlement Arrival → Wedding)

**Keep everything liquid. Keep it in her name.**

The settlement arrives 6–9 months before marriage. During that window, the capital is legally hers. Clean ownership protects both parties. Nothing goes into Kerry's entities until they are married — not because of distrust, but because proper structure is what protects both of them if anything unexpected happens.

### Actions

**1. Open a high-yield savings account in her name immediately.**
Park the full settlement here upon arrival. Target: 4.5–5% APY. Current T-bill rates offer similar or better returns on 3-month instruments if she wants a slightly higher yield with the same safety.

**2. Get CPA confirmation on tax treatment.**
Have her attorney's settlement breakdown document ready. Walk through it with a CPA before treating the full $150k as the working number.

**3. Begin entity and legal buildout — funded from the settlement.**
This is the one pre-marriage deployment that makes sense. Waiting on legal structure costs months of foundation time. This is a shared benefit she is knowingly contributing to, pre-marriage. Document it clearly.

**4. Begin researching whole life designers.**
Do not purchase anything yet. Use this window to interview 2–3 advanced-market insurance designers (not general agents) and get stress-tested policy illustrations. Know exactly what you're buying before the money is ready to deploy.

## Critical Rules for This Capital

1. **Her name until married.** No exceptions. Clean ownership protects both parties.
2. **CPA signs off on tax treatment before you plan around the full number.**
3. **Notes don't get funded until underwriting is sharp.** Two or three coached note deals through mentorship first. The $50k waits.
4. **Whole life premium must be sustainable on income.** If you can't carry the premium indefinitely from Kerry's paycheck and SB Creations, size it down. The policy only works if it doesn't lapse.
5. **Emergency reserve is not investable capital.** It doesn't go into notes, crypto, deals, or anything with any risk. It sits in a HYSA and earns yield while doing its real job: staying available.
6. **Baby expenses are not in this plan.** Day-to-day baby costs come from income. This capital does not absorb lifestyle costs of any kind.
7. **No lifestyle upgrades from this money.** This is foundation capital. Treat it differently than everything else.
