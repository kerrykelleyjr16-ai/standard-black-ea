## 2. Layer-by-Layer Breakdown

### Layer 0 — Kelley Family Dynasty Trust (Tennessee)
**The permanent vault. Everything answers to this.**

- Tennessee is the best jurisdiction: no rule against perpetuities, strong asset protection, low cost.
- Owns Kasino Family Holdings LLC (the holding company).
- Holds trust-owned whole life insurance policies (Family Bank source capital).
- Houses the family constitution: mission, governance rules, succession plan, beneficiary framework.
- Trustee controls policy actions and major allocations per trust terms.
- Trust Protector (separate person or committee) can replace trustee and approve major changes.

**What it does NOT do:** daily operations, deal-level management, payroll.

---

### Layer 1 — Kasino Family Holdings LLC
**The parent company. Controls the whole enterprise.**

- Already exists as the named holding entity — keep it, formalize it.
- Owns membership interests in all sub-entities below.
- Clean holding structure: no direct operations, no employee payroll, no customer-facing contracts.
- Separate bank account, separate books.

---

### Layer 2 — Standard Black Family Office Management LLC
**Runs the system. The operational brain.**

- Handles: bookkeeping and reporting, advisor coordination, deal administration, budgeting, dashboards, legal/tax/insurance coordination, team payroll and contractors.
- Receives a management fee from Kasino Family Holdings.
- As Standard Black grows, this becomes the formal family office.
- Currently: Kerry functions as this. When Kody takes operating CEO role, this entity is what he runs.

---

### Layer 3 — Kelley Family Bank LLC
**The internal lending platform. Funded by whole life cash value.**

**How the banking loop works:**

1. Trust makes premium payments into whole life policies (funded by Kerry's income, business cash flow, or gift transfers over time).
2. Policies build cash value — guaranteed, tax-advantaged growth.
3. When capital is needed, trustee authorizes a policy loan.
4. Family Bank LLC deploys those funds as loans per the lending policy.
5. Borrowers (family members, entities within the structure) repay with interest.
6. Interest income recycles back into: new premiums, operating reserves, or reinvestment.

**Phase 1 lending targets (internal only):**
- Bridge loans for note deal deposits and closings
- Entity formation and legal costs
- Operating capital for Standard Black Creations
- Real estate note acquisition advances

**Phase 2+ lending targets (as policies scale):**
- Expanded note acquisitions
- Operating company acquisitions
- Eventually outside parties (with counsel and compliance)

**Hard guardrails (non-negotiable):**
- Policy loan cap: 60–70% of cash value system-wide
- 12–24 month premium reserve in cash/T-bills at all times
- Annual in-force illustration and loan stress test

---

### Layer 4 — Kelley Capital Management LLC
**The fund manager. Earns fees and carry on investor capital.**

- Manager/GP entity for Note Fund I and any future funds.
- Receives: management fees, acquisition fees, asset management fees, carried interest.
- Keeps fund economics separate from personal/family holdings.
- Critical: once outside investor money enters, this becomes a securities matter — engage fund counsel before raising.

---

### Layer 5 — Note Fund I LLC (and SPVs)


- Phase 1 (now → 2 years): family capital only. No outside investors. No compliance requirements.
- Phase 2 (3–5 years): raise outside LP capital. Formal fund documents, subscription agreements, distribution waterfall, reporting.
- Keep family-owned note pools in separate SPVs from investor fund pools.

---

### Layer 6 — Standard Black Holdings LLC
**The brand platform. Owns the Standard Black operating businesses.**

```
Standard Black Holdings LLC
    |
    |---- Standard Black Creations LLC (website dev — ACTIVE, generating revenue)
    |---- [Standard Black brand extensions as they launch]
```

- Separate from the investment side by design.
- Cash flow from SB Creations eventually seeds Family Bank premiums and reserves.
- Future SB brands (media, retail, services) sit as separate LLCs inside here.

---

### Layer 7 — Kelley Operating Holdings LLC
**Future operating company acquisitions. Separate from everything else.**

```
Kelley Operating Holdings LLC

    |---- Property Management Acquisition LLC

```

- Acquisitions don't happen until Phase III (6–10 years).
- Each acquired company gets its own LLC: separate books, bank accounts, insurance, payroll, contracts.
- Operating company risk stays fully isolated from the investment portfolio.

## 3. The Treasury Reserve (Non-Negotiable Foundation)

Before aggressive investing, the treasury must be funded and maintained.

**Treasury covers:**
- Personal emergency fund (6 months minimum)
- Business operating overhead and payroll
- Taxes (quarterly estimates)
- Insurance premiums (whole life + business coverage)
- Legal and entity admin costs
- Note deal reserves (workout advances, property expenses on NPLs)
- Opportunity fund (capital ready to move on deals)

**Where it lives:** dedicated account under Kasino Family Holdings or the Family Office Management LLC.

**Rule:** if treasury drops below minimum thresholds, all discretionary investment activity pauses until it's replenished.

## 4. Cash Flow Map

### Family investment side (notes, real estate):
```
Note/RE assets produce payments
    → Asset SPLLCs receive income
    → Expenses, reserves, servicer fees paid first
    → Net cash to Kasino Family Holdings
    → Treasury replenished to target
    → Excess reinvested: new policies, new deals, or distributed to trust
```

### Standard Black operating side:
```
SB Creations invoices clients
    → Revenue into Standard Black Holdings LLC
    → Operating expenses paid
    → Net cash flows to Kasino Family Holdings
    → Directed to treasury or Family Bank premiums
```

### Family Bank loop:
```
Premium payments (from income or entity distributions)
    → Whole life cash value builds
    → Policy loans taken when capital needed
    → Deployed as loans through Family Bank LLC
    → Interest collected → recycled into premiums and reserves
```

### Future fund (outside investors):
```
LP capital raised
    → Note Fund I deploys into note pools
    → Borrower payments collected
    → Servicer fees, expenses, reserves paid first
    → LP preferred return
    → Return of LP capital
    → Remaining profits split: LPs + GP carry to Kelley Capital Management
```

## 8. Non-Negotiable Rules

1. One entity, one bank account, one set of books. No exceptions.
2. Treasury reserve is funded before any new investment capital is deployed.
3. Family-only note pools stay separate from outside-investor fund pools forever.
4. No operating company risk mixes with the passive investment portfolio.
5. No outside investor capital without securities attorney and proper fund documents.
6. Family Bank loan-to-cash-value never exceeds 70% system-wide.
7. Annual policy health review and loan stress test — mandatory, not optional.
8. Every meaningful decision goes in the decision log.
9. Written intercompany agreements between all related entities. No informal transfers.
10. Operating companies (HVAC, construction, etc.) get their own everything: books, bank accounts, insurance, payroll, management reporting.
